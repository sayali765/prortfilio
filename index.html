Inten at Vinz Global

Firstly, I was really happy when our TPO shared that five of us were selected for the internship out of 25 people. I was able to secure my seat. Then, we were informed that the date was October 16, 2023, and I was delighted to learn that my mentor was Krunal, sir. He made me comfortable with all the IT stuff and gradually updated me. I feel that having the right mentor for our career is important, and I found that in him. I started with DBMS management system. As the exam approached, we decided to take a break. In January 2023, we started again, but now the game changed; as there were five of us, they formed us into a team and provided us with the CV fraud detection system.

Firstly, we had to include all the colleges in India in our search. Users could type in "IIT" or "Indian Institute," and we had to conduct the search accordingly. This took a little more time because the technologies mentioned were Java, Spring Boot, and for frontend, ReactJS, which were new to us. But as time went on, we were able to extract the email IDs, phone numbers, and other information from the resumes. However, some resume formats were different, so it took 2-3 days to figure out how to extract the names from them. Regarding phone numbers, there were 8 to 9 different formats, so we used regular expressions. Eventually, we were able to extract names, phone numbers, email IDs, college names, skills, and experiences. This is still a work in progress and will continue to grow.

Consulant

What Happens When You Work with Dedication? Opportunities Come Your Way! Here's a real-life example of how dedication can open doors to incredible opportunities. Recently, I crossed paths with my junior, Nishe Services. Yes, you heard that right! He was eager to grow his business but needed help with operations, social media presence, and employee management. We had an insightful conversation where he shared his concerns about hiring the right people for his company. He was worried about whether the employees he hired would be committed to their tasks and deliver within deadlines. I advised him that while hiring someone is essential, it's not just about filling a position—it's about ensuring that the person you bring on board is truly dedicated to your vision.

I suggested creating documentation and a legal contract outlining expectations, salary increments, and reductions based on performance. This way, both parties are clear on their commitments. Start by hiring one employee and conducting regular performance reviews, like a Scrum analysis, to gauge whether your company is on track for success. As you gain confidence and see progress, you can gradually expand your team. I also shared some tips and tricks on how to build a strong, committed team that aligns with your business goals. It's all about starting small, analyzing the outcomes, and then scaling up.
